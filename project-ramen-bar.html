Ramen Bar Website Redesign

For my first design project as a UI/UX student (and also the first ever project in my life), I was assigned to redesign an existing website to fix its interface design problems. I decided to kick off with the website of Ramen Bar - a local Japanese restaurant in University City, Philadelphia - as I had already fallen in love with their amazing foods. As a customer, I used to get frustrated with some of their website’s design problems, so as a designer, I wanted to propose some solutions to enhance the site’s user experience. Getting started with something familiar gave me some confidence and enabled me to make mistakes and learn in a “safe” zone.

1. Project Plan

I firstly set up a detailed plan and gathered foundational information needed to guide me throughout the project.

  • User Persona & Main User Tasks: These two tasks were accomplished based on my own experience as a customer and interviews with other people around me who can be the target audience of the restaurant. Defining the activities users perform the most on this website gave me an idea of the site’s most important functions - what I needed to concentrate my design on.
  • Analysis of the original website: Despite owning a strong color palette for branding, the website is often seen as a dry and monotonous visual evidence of the restaurant, as the text overpower visual elements and few beneficial functions to satisfy the customers’ needs. Therefore, I planned to develop on its existing palette and refine the layout in the redesign version. I also evaluated how the original website works on different device screens to identify potential problems with responsiveness.
  • Project goals: As a result of my analysis and research, I listed out the redesign goals. These goals were carefully deliberated to fit the target audience’s demands and solve the existing problems of the website.

2. Information Architecture & Research

I came up with every kind of content that can and should be on the restaurant’s website, then created a card sorting exercise for six people representing the target audience to complete. Curious about different people’s perspectives, I had a short interview with each person after the exercise to investigate what kind of content they expect to see on the site, the reasoning on their way of categorizing information. Their answers gave me some interesting insights as they could be different from my perspective, even though all of us were the target audience. I carried out a data analysis of the exercise and laid out a site map demonstrating the most suitable hierarchy that helps users navigate the website.

3. Wireframing

This is my favorite part of the project, in which I searched for inspiration, brainstormed ideas, and explored every potential layout and style for the site. In the first half of the project in which I focused on designing for the desktop, I worked on the three most important pages: homepage, menu page, and contact page. I sketched out multiple wireframes for each page while keeping the research results in mind: how to arrange the information based on users’ expectations while still highlighting the restaurant’s features. In the end, I asked several potential users for feedback then finalized one wireframe for each page. I also created a new color scheme for the site, inspired by the image of sushi.
